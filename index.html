Publications

AgentSuite: Toward More Reliable Agent Evaluation with a Component-Based Benchmark Auditing Pipeline

H. Suh*, S. Lee*, B. Ji*, R. Khare, B. Khan, H. Kim, Tianyi Zhang, et al. (* equal contribution)

ICML 2026 Paper

A component-based pipeline that audits validity flaws in LLM-agent benchmarks — decomposing tasks into user, environment, ground-truth, and evaluation components. It matches expert judgments at 0.79–0.87 F1 across 6 benchmarks, finds flaws affecting ~23% of tasks, and reshuffles 63% of model rankings on a 30-LLM leaderboard.

Causal Brain Connectivity: Integrating Granger Directed Graphs in fMRI Analysis

Tianyi Zhang, K. Han, J. Nie, et al.

AIME 2025 Paper

A causal method that recovers directional brain-connectivity dependencies invisible to correlation-based graphs, improving downstream diagnostic prediction across ~8K patients via Granger-directed graphs.

Brain Network State Transformer: Leveraging State Functional Connectivity

J. Nie, K. Han, Tianyi Zhang, et al.

IEEE EMBC 2025 · Oral Paper

A state-based Transformer that models temporal transitions across latent functional-connectivity states, outperforming static-connectivity baselines on clinical prediction.

Projects

monitorstress

In progress Code

An adversarial stress-test suite for LLM-based safety monitors — the class of system labs like METR and UK AISI use to detect agent reward hacking and sandbagging. Built on a typed Python/Pydantic-v2 stack, it applies semantics-preserving transformations (chain-of-thought stripping, observation truncation, action-padding noise) to agent transcripts from METR's MALT split and measures how monitor discrimination degrades — removing a model's reasoning traces collapses a Claude-Sonnet-based monitor from AUROC 0.683 to chance.
